About Me

Hello, I'm Prahlad Kumar, the proud owner of Sirohi Goat Farming and Breeding Setup. I have a deep-rooted passion for sustainable agriculture, livestock management, and animal welfare. At Shri Brihd Goat Farm, we focus on high-quality breeding practices to ensure the health and productivity of our goats. We offer expertise in goat farming techniques, from feeding and healthcare to breeding strategies. Our mission is to support fellow farmers and the agricultural community by sharing knowledge and resources that drive growth and success.

The Sirohi goat is a hardy breed originating from the Sirohi district of Rajasthan, India. Known for its resilience in arid and semi-arid regions, it is primarily raised for milk and meat production. Sirohi goats are medium-sized with distinctive horns and a lively temperament. They are an important part of the rural economy, valued for their adaptability and productivity.

Exploring Our Sirohi Goat Farm : A Journey into Quality Livestock Farming

Welcome to our Sirohi goat farm, where tradition meets modern farming techniques. Our farm specializes in rearing Sirohi goats, known for their high-quality milk, meat, and adaptability to various climates. In this blog, we'll walk you through our farming practices, the benefits of the Sirohi breed, and tips for potential goat farmers.

Farming Practices :

We focus on sustainable farming methods, ensuring the health and well-being of our goats. Our goats are raised in a free-range environment with ample space and natural forage. We also integrate veterinary care and regular health check-ups to maintain optimal conditions.

Benefits of the Sirohi Goat :

Sirohi goats are renowned for their prolific breeding and strong resistance to diseases. They have good milk production, rich in fat and protein—ideal for dairy products—along with tender and high-quality meat. Their adaptability makes them suitable for various terrains and climates.

Tips for Goat Farmers :

For those considering goat farming, start by ensuring proper shelter and feeding. Focus on breed selection—Sirohi goats are a great choice—and practice regular health monitoring.
