Sirohi & Ajmera Breed :

The Sirohi goat is a hardy breed originating from the Ajmer district of Rajasthan, India. Known for its resilience in arid and semi-arid regions, it is primarily raised for milk and meat production. Sirohi goats are medium-sized with distinctive horns and a lively temperament. They are an important part of the rural economy, valued for their adaptability and productivity.

Sojat Breed :

The Sojat goat breed is known for its adaptability and economic value, particularly in Rajasthan. It features long ears, short tails, and conical teats. Males weigh around 50–60 kg, females around 40–50 kg. It's primarily raised for meat due to its quality and resilience in dry climates.

Gujari Breed :

The Gujari (or Gujri) is a hardy goat breed primarily found in Rajasthan, India, known for its dual-purpose value in both meat and milk production. These goats are valued for their adaptability and good milk yield, and they are a popular choice for meat. Gujari goats come in various colors and have a sturdy build, with an average weight of around 25-30 kilograms.
